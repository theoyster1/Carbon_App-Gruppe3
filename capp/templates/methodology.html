General emissions formulas

We are going to studty some \(\LaTeX ~ formulas\) that could be useful for our project:

    General emissions

    The general formula used to calculate the users emissions, is the following:
    \[CO^2 \: per \: km = {\frac{F*C}{V} + \frac{L}{T} \over P}*x \]
    Where:

    • \(F\) is the fuel comsumption expressed in Quantity of Energy per km (\(Qe/km\)). \(Qe\) being the quantity of energy expressed in volume (e.g. litre), mass (e.g. kg) og energy unit (e.g. KWh)

    • \(C\) is the mass of CO2 (kg) per Quantity of Energy \(kg/Qe\), \(Qe\) being expressed in the same unit as for \(F\)

    • \(V\) is the vehicle efficiency factor, which is allways eaqual to \(1\)

    • \(L\) is the lifecycle carbon emission of the vehicle

    • \(T\) is the average kilometer amount the vehicle has treveled

    • \(P\) is the average amount of people present in the vehicle

    • \(x\) is the kilometer amount



Emissions by transport type



    Cars

    The carbon emissions for cars are seperated into four categories, with differend emissions. They all have P = 1,5, but differ in the other constants:

  1. Gasoline: \(F = 0,0561\), \(C = 2,88\), \(L = 5 600\), \(T = 160 000\)
  2. Diesel: \(F = 0,053\), \(C = 3,24\), \(L = 5 600\), \(T = 208 000\)
  3. Compressed NG: \(F = 1,229\), \(C = 3,07\), \(L = 5 600\), \(T = 184 000\)
  4. Electric: \(F = 0\), \(C = 0,371\), \(L = 8 800\), \(T = 321868\)


    5. Emissions per km travelled by car (Gasoline): \[CO^2 = {\frac{0,0561*2,88}{1} + \frac{5600}{160000} \over 1,5}*x =0,13/km\]


    Bus

    The carbon emissions for busses are seperated into three categories, with differend emissions. They all have P = 56, but differ in the other constants:

  1. Diesel: \(F = 0,326\), \(C = 3,24\), \(L = 140000\), \(T = 450320\)
  2. Comp bio-diesel: \(F = 0,41\), \(C = 1,92\), \(L = 140000\), \(T = 450320\)
  3. Electric: \(F = 0\), \(C = 0,371\), \(L = 150000\), \(T = 320000\)



    4. Emissions per km travelled by bus (diesel): \[CO^2 = {\frac{0,326*3,24}{1} + \frac{140000}{450320} \over 56}*x = 0,024/km\]



    Passenger train

    The carbon emissions for passenger trains are seperated into two categories, with differend emissions. They all have P = 350, but differ in the other constants:

  1. Diesel: \(F = 4,25\), \(C = 3,24\), \(L = 1656700\), \(T = 7724851\)
  2. Electric: \(F = 0\), \(C = 0,371\), \(L = 1656700\), \(T = 7724851\)


    3. Emissions per km travelled by train (diesel): \[CO^2 = {\frac{4,25*3,24}{1} + \frac{1656700}{7724851} \over 350}*x = 0,04/km \]


    Airplane

    The carbon emissions for airplanes are are all the same, with P = 121,55, because all aiplanes currently use jet fuel:

  1. Jet fuel: \(F = 3,8\), \(C = 3,1\), \(L = 61000\), \(T = 84490560\)




    2. Emissions per km travelled by airplane: \[CO^2 = {\frac{3,8*3,1}{1} + \frac{61000}{84490560} \over 121,55}*x = 0,09/km \]



    Motorcycle

    The carbon emissions for motorcycles are all the same in our calculator, because all motorcycles currently use gasoline. They all have P = 1,5, and following other constants:

  1. Gasoline: \(F = 0,041\), \(C = 2,88\), \(L = 2000\), \(T = 200000\)


    2. Emissions per km travelled by motorcycle: \[CO^2 = {\frac{0,041*2,88}{1} + \frac{2000}{200000} \over 1,5}*x = 0,08/km \]


    Boat

    The carbon emissions for boats are all the same in our calculator, because all boats currently use diesel. They all have P = 5, and following other constants:

  1. Diesel: \(F = 0,784\), \(C = 3,24\), \(L = 553000\), \(T = 64373\)


    2. Emissions per km travelled by boat: \[CO^2 = {\frac{0,784*3,24}{1} + \frac{553000}{64373} \over 5}*x = 2,226 /km\]
